Use case: JMTY factory / manufacturing job recruitment banner
Asset type: 1:1 Japanese raster image banner, PNG output, optimized for smartphone readability

Create a high-impact Japanese job banner for a factory or light manufacturing role.
The design should borrow only the reference image's energetic advertising feel: bold oversized typography, bright pink accent color, diagonal movement, dense but readable composition, and strong salary emphasis.
Do not reuse the reference image's at-home/SNS/video-editing theme, icons, laptop-only setting, anime illustration style, or flat vector-ad appearance.

Main text placeholders:
- Largest salary text: 「{{salary_text}}」
- Main role text: 「{{role_phrase}}」
- Region text: 「{{region}}」
- Short support copy: 「未経験OK」
- Optional condition chips if accurate to the post: 「寮費無料」「土日休み」「軽作業」「日払い相談可」

Required visual hierarchy:
1. Put 「{{salary_text}}」 in the largest and highest-contrast type. It must be readable first on a phone.
2. Put 「{{role_phrase}}」 as the second-largest headline, clearly tied to factory/manufacturing work.
3. Add 「{{region}}」 in a smaller but still legible badge or label.
4. Use only 2-4 short benefit labels. Keep all Japanese text brief and crisp.

Layout and composition:
- Square 1:1 banner with a dynamic diagonal layout.
- Left or lower-left area: bold salary block with thick white outline or high-contrast dark outline.
- Upper-left or center-left area: large role headline 「{{role_phrase}}」.
- Right side or background: photo-real or semi-realistic factory scene showing clean manufacturing equipment, inspection work, assembly line, machine operation, protective clothing, gloves, parts trays, conveyor, metal/plastic materials, and realistic lighting.
- Use a layered ad look with solid text panels, angled ribbons, and small condition chips, but keep those graphic elements secondary to the realistic workplace background.
- Keep enough margins so large Japanese text is not cropped.

Visual style:
- Energetic pink accent inspired by the reference image, balanced with industrial whites, dark charcoal, clean gray, metallic silver, and safety yellow accents.
- Bright, clean, high-contrast lighting. The factory should feel safe, modern, and practical, not dark or abstract.
- Typography should be bold, blocky, Japanese display-ad style, with thick outlines and strong shadows only where they improve readability.
- Dense commercial banner energy is acceptable, but every text element must remain legible at small size.

Factory-specific content direction:
- The image must clearly look like a factory / manufacturing / inspection / assembly / machine-operation job.
- Use a realistic or semi-realistic raster background with depth, texture, equipment, people or hands working, and actual workplace atmosphere.
- Match the job post details. Do not invent benefits that conflict with the post.
- If the role is broad, show a neutral clean light-manufacturing workspace rather than a specific real company or branded facility.

Strict avoid rules:
- Do not include 「クリックして」「ボタンで」「LINEで」 or any button-like strong CTA as the main message.
- Do not include LINE URLs, QR codes, real company names, logos, watermarks, social media icons, app icons, or platform logos.
- Do not use 「完全在宅」「出勤不要」「スマホだけ」 or any copy that makes the job look like remote work.
- Do not show a home office, laptop-only work, video editing, influencer/SNS work, side-job mood, or casual cafe workspace as the main subject.
- Do not create SVG, vector art, flat illustration, line art, icon-only layouts, geometric template ads, or Figma/Canva-style vector banners.
- Even if the file is PNG/JPG, the appearance must not look like a vector-only template. The background must have photographic or semi-realistic texture, lighting, and depth.
- Do not overload the banner with tiny text. Prioritize salary, role, region, and a few short conditions.
