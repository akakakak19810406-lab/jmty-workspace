Use case: ads-marketing
Asset type: 1:1 Japanese job recruitment banner for Jimoty

Primary request:
Create a polished Japanese recruitment banner for {{role_phrase}} in {{region}}.
The banner should feel like a premium semi-realistic illustrated job ad, with a dreamy but trustworthy atmosphere and a clear salary-first message.

Main text to include in Japanese:
- Primary salary text, largest and most visible: 「{{salary_text}}」
- Job role headline: 「{{role_phrase}}」
- Short trust/entry phrase: 「未経験OK」
- Optional region text when useful: 「{{region}}」
- Optional short benefit chips: 「在宅OK」「WワークOK」「自分のペースで」

Visual style:
Use a raster, semi-realistic illustrated scene with real depth, light, texture, and atmosphere. A clean evening or night workspace mood works well: soft moonlight, warm desk light, subtle stars, a laptop or work tools, plants or room details, and a calm approachable person or work scene. The background must feel like a real illustrated environment, not a flat graphic layout.

Layout and hierarchy:
- Square 1:1 composition optimized for smartphone readability.
- Put {{salary_text}} in the strongest visual position, very large, high contrast, and readable at thumbnail size.
- Place {{role_phrase}} as the main job headline above or near the salary, using elegant Japanese typography.
- Use only a few short supporting phrases; avoid long sentences.
- Use small rounded chips, translucent panels, or soft ribbon labels only as supporting elements.
- Keep the layout rich and premium, but ensure the text hierarchy is simple: salary first, role second, benefits third.
- Leave enough contrast behind every text area so Japanese characters stay crisp.

Typography:
Use elegant Japanese Mincho-style or refined serif lettering for the main headline, paired with clean readable Gothic-style text for smaller labels. The salary numerals should be bold, luminous, and highly legible, with a soft glow or shadow if needed. Avoid broken Japanese typography, distorted glyphs, cramped line spacing, or tiny decorative text.

Color direction:
Use deep navy, moonlit blue, warm cream, soft ivory, pale pink, and muted gold accents. Keep the image bright enough to read on a phone. Use high contrast for {{salary_text}} and main copy, with warm highlights against a darker atmospheric background.

Density:
Moderately high visual density is acceptable, with detailed background texture and premium decorative accents, but do not overcrowd the copy. Decorative stars, light arcs, ribbons, and subtle panels may be used sparingly to frame the message.

Required avoidance:
- Do not make the image look like SVG, vector art, flat illustration, line art, icon art, a shape-only template, or a Figma/Canva-style vector advertisement.
- Do not create a banner made only from geometric shapes, icons, or flat colored panels.
- Do not use HTML, SVG, XML, code output, or diagram-like construction.
- Do not include LINE URLs, QR codes, real company names, watermarks, logos, or fake app buttons.
- Do not make 「クリックして」「ボタンで」「LINEで」 or similar strong CTA/button-like phrases the main message.
- Do not use a large CTA button as the visual focus.
- Do not contradict the job post text.
- Do not bury {{salary_text}} in small text.

Output requirement:
Generate as a crisp PNG-style raster image. Prioritize the appeal and readability of the banner itself, with clear Japanese text and strong contrast.
