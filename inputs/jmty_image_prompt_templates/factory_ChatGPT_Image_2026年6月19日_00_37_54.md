Use case: ads-marketing
Asset type: 1:1 Japanese job recruitment banner for Jimoty

Create a high-impact Japanese job recruitment banner in the style of an intense printed manga magazine advertisement, using a raster, photo-textured or semi-realistic illustrated background. The banner must feel energetic, bold, and readable on a smartphone, while keeping the job offer clear and trustworthy.

Main copy to include:
- Primary role headline: 「{{role_phrase}}」
- Highest-priority salary text: 「{{salary_text}}」
- Short support copy: 「未経験OK」
- Optional region/context label when it does not conflict with the post text: 「{{region}}」

Visual style:
- Dramatic manga / gekiga-inspired recruitment banner with thick black outlines, rough ink texture, halftone grain, paint splashes, speed lines, glow, sparks, and high-contrast lighting.
- The background should have real-world depth and material texture: a factory, worksite, office, or job-relevant scene rendered as a semi-realistic raster image, not a flat graphic panel.
- Use a strong red, yellow, black, and white palette with small accents of blue or purple lighting, similar to a dense magazine cover or energetic job ad.
- Add a central worker or job-relevant person when appropriate. The person may be semi-realistic manga style, expressive, confident, and approachable, but should not look like a corporate mascot or simple icon.
- Money, tools, workplace details, lights, steam, motion lines, or paper-like texture may be used as supporting elements, as long as they do not hide the text.

Layout and typography:
- Square 1:1 composition, designed for mobile readability.
- Make 「{{salary_text}}」 the largest and most prominent element, with very high contrast, thick black/white outline, red-to-yellow heat gradient, and enough spacing around the numerals.
- Place 「{{role_phrase}}」 as a large secondary headline near the top or upper-left, using bold Japanese display lettering with distressed ink texture.
- Keep supporting text short. Use only one or two compact callout blocks such as 「未経験OK」 or a brief benefit phrase that matches the post.
- Use vertical or diagonal manga-style callouts only if they remain easy to read. Avoid cluttering the banner with many small phrases.
- Text should be sharp, not warped, not misspelled, and not cut off at the edges.

Content rules:
- The image must prioritize the offer itself: job type, salary, and beginner-friendly message.
- Do not make 「クリックして」「ボタンで」「LINEで」 or similar action-button CTA wording the main message.
- Do not include LINE URLs, QR codes, real company names, watermarks, platform logos, or actual contact information.
- Do not contradict the source post text. If {{region}} is used, it must match the post context.
- Keep all Japanese text short, bold, and readable at thumbnail size.

Avoid:
- SVG, vector art, flat illustration, line-art-only visuals, icon-only layouts, Figma/Canva-style geometric ad templates, or banners that look assembled only from shapes.
- Generic "use the reference image" wording as the final prompt.
- Tiny text, excessive benefit lists, low contrast, pale colors, clean minimal corporate design, cute mascot tone, QR codes, fake buttons, UI-like CTA areas, logos, watermarks, and broken Japanese typography.

Output requirement:
- Generate a raster PNG image. The final image should look like a dense, dramatic manga-style recruitment banner with a textured real-world job scene and crisp Japanese lettering.
