Use case: ads-marketing
Asset type: 1:1 Japanese job recruitment banner for Jimoty factory/manufacturing posts

Create a high-impact Japanese factory recruitment banner with the look of a bold weekly job-ad magazine cover. The image must feel like a raster photo-based ad, not a vector template.

Main Japanese text to include:
- Primary salary: 「{{salary_text}}」
- Job role: 「{{role_phrase}}」
- Region: 「{{region}}」
- Short trust/entry phrase: 「未経験OK」
- Optional short benefits if they match the post: 「土日休み」「長期休暇あり」「簡単作業」「研修あり」「交通費支給」「寮費無料」

Recommended copy hierarchy:
1. Make 「{{salary_text}}」 the largest and clearest information, placed near the center-right or upper-right. Use very large yellow or white numerals with thick black outline and a bright blue backing panel.
2. Show 「{{role_phrase}}」 as the factory job identity, large enough to read on a phone. It may be vertical on the right edge or a strong block near the top.
3. Put 「{{region}}」 close to the role or salary in a compact badge or local map-like accent, without using a real logo or official map data.
4. Place benefit chips along the bottom as short readable labels. Keep each benefit to 4-8 Japanese characters when possible.

Visual style:
- Use a photo-real or semi-real factory/manufacturing workspace as the base scene: clean production line, machinery, inspection bench, assembly area, safety-conscious lighting, depth of field, metallic texture, and visible workplace atmosphere.
- Include one positive factory worker in workwear, helmet or cap, placed left or center-left. The person should look realistic or semi-realistic, not cartoon, flat illustration, or icon-like.
- Dense, energetic weekly-ad layout with strong contrast, thick black strokes, white keylines, slight drop shadows, red/yellow/blue color blocks, and gritty printed texture.
- Color balance should emphasize vivid red, bright yellow, deep blue, black, and white. Avoid a pale pastel look.
- Typography should be bold Japanese display lettering, heavy Gothic style, high contrast, readable at smartphone size. Avoid broken Japanese, warped characters, tiny legal text, or too many long sentences.
- Use angled panels, sticker-like badges, and framed chips only as supporting elements. The real photo-based factory/person scene must remain visible behind the text.

Layout guidance:
- Square 1:1 composition, PNG output, crisp raster text.
- Top area: urgent headline or role/salary framing, but do not make a click/LINE action the headline.
- Center: worker and factory scene with the salary as the main visual hook.
- Right side: vertical or stacked 「{{role_phrase}}」 and/or manufacturing category wording such as 「工場勤務」「製造」「検査」「組立」 only if consistent with the post.
- Bottom band: 3-5 benefit chips with simple icons only if they do not dominate. Text remains more important than icons.
- Keep text short, layered, and readable. Prefer a few strong phrases over a paragraph.

Factory-only content rules:
- The banner must clearly look like a factory/manufacturing/light-work job, not a remote-work, smartphone-only, side-job, or home-based job.
- Do not include phrases such as 「完全在宅」「出勤不要」「スマホだけ」.
- Use factory-related scene cues and words only when they match the post: manufacturing, inspection, assembly, light work, machine operation, clean production line, training, stable work.
- Salary, region, holidays, dormitory, transportation, and other conditions must not contradict the post data.

Avoid:
- No CTA-style main claims such as 「クリックして」「ボタンで」「LINEで」.
- No LINE URL, QR code, real company name, real logo, watermark, or brand mark.
- No SVG, HTML, XML, code-generated image, vector art, flat illustration, line art, icon-led design, Figma/Canva-like geometric template, or a banner made only from shapes and text.
- Do not make the background a black solid field with only text and panels. The factory workspace must have photo-like realism, lighting, texture, and depth.
- Do not use a generic instruction like "use the reference image"; this template should stand alone for future JMTY factory banners.
