Use case: ads-marketing
Asset type: 1:1 Japanese job recruitment banner for Jimoty

Primary request:
Create a bright, high-impact Japanese remote-work job banner for {{role_phrase}} in {{region}}. The banner must look like a polished raster advertisement built from a semi-realistic home-office scene, not a flat vector template.

Required main copy:
- 「完全在宅」
- 「{{role_phrase}}」
- 「{{salary_text}}」
- 「未経験OK」
- Include {{region}} only if it matches the posting context and does not make the job look location-bound.

Visual style:
Use a warm, cheerful, semi-realistic 3D/raster look with a cozy home desk, laptop or desktop monitor, soft daylight, curtains, plants, coffee cup, notebook, and a person doing calm PC work. The atmosphere should feel approachable, modern, and safe for home-based work. Use glossy dimensional Japanese text with thick white outlines, soft shadows, and rounded sticker-like badges. Keep the overall look energetic and dense like a Japanese recruitment banner, while preserving clear reading order on a phone.

Layout:
Use a 1:1 square composition. Put the biggest text block in the upper-left to center area: 「{{role_phrase}}」 as the main role headline. Put 「{{salary_text}}」 below it in the largest or second-largest type, using very high contrast so the salary is immediately readable. Place 「完全在宅」 in a prominent red or pink rounded badge near the top-left. Add a smaller blue speech-bubble or cloud badge for 「未経験OK」 or a short beginner-friendly note. Keep the right side or background anchored by the home PC work scene, with the person and monitor visible but not covering the main salary text.

Typography and hierarchy:
Use short Japanese phrases only. Make 「{{salary_text}}」 the strongest salary anchor with oversized numerals, red or pink fill, thick white outline, and drop shadow. Use blue for the main role headline and red/pink for salary and important badges. Supporting text should be much smaller but still readable on a smartphone. Avoid long sentences; use compact benefit chips such as 「PC作業中心」「マニュアルあり」「自分のペース」 only when they fit cleanly.

Color and density:
Base the palette on warm cream daylight, clean white outlines, saturated royal blue, vivid red/pink, and small yellow accent highlights. Use rounded badges, speech bubbles, ribbons, and soft panel areas sparingly to organize text. The image can be information-rich, but every text group must have enough contrast and breathing room.

Remote-work content direction:
The job must clearly read as a remote-work banner. Show home-based PC work, online support, spreadsheet-like work, AI assistance, data entry, or office support. The scene placeholder can be interpreted as: {{scene_phrase}}. If {{scene_phrase}} is empty, use a modern home desk with laptop, spreadsheet-like work, calm daylight, and a friendly remote-work mood.

Avoid:
Do not use strong CTA phrases as the main appeal, including 「クリックして」「ボタンで」「LINEで」. Do not include LINE URLs, QR codes, real company names, logos, watermarks, or misleading posting claims. Do not make the design look like a factory job: avoid factories, production lines, uniforms, helmets, warehouses, and manufacturing equipment as the main subject. Do not use SVG, vector art, flat illustration, line art, icon-only design, geometric template ads, Canva/Figma-style vector banners, HTML/XML/code output, or a design made mostly from shapes. The background and work scene must have raster texture, lighting, depth, and realistic or semi-realistic material detail.

Output quality:
Generate a PNG-style raster image with sharp Japanese text, clean contrast, no broken typography, no unreadable microtext, and no contradiction with the posting text for role, salary, region, and work conditions.
