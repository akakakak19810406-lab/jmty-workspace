Use case: ads-marketing
Asset type: 1:1 Japanese job recruitment banner for Jimoty

Create a high-impact Japanese job banner for 「{{role_phrase}}」. The banner must make 「{{salary_text}}」 the strongest visual priority, followed by 「{{role_phrase}}」 and short trust/entry points such as 「未経験OK」. Include 「{{region}}」 only as a small context label when the job post needs area information; do not let the region overpower the salary or role.

Visual style:
- Energetic Japanese pop recruitment banner with a bright yellow base, deep black contrast shapes, hot pink salary accents, and cyan/white support areas.
- Dense but controlled composition inspired by bold magazine/job-ad graphics: diagonal panels, cropped geometric accent pieces, dotted texture, and strong layered depth.
- Use raster, photo-like or semi-realistic visual texture. Background, person, work scene, clothing, lighting, and shadows must feel like a real image or polished semi-real raster illustration, not a flat vector template.
- If a person is shown, place them on the right side or lower-right edge with a friendly, approachable expression and a dynamic pose. Keep the person secondary to the salary text.
- Match the job category visually: show subtle work-related context for {{role_phrase}} through realistic props, workspace hints, or clothing, without using company logos or real brand marks.

Layout:
- Square 1:1 composition for smartphone viewing.
- Top area: large role headline, e.g. 「{{role_phrase}}」, in bold black Japanese gothic type on a yellow field.
- Center-left: oversized salary block with 「{{salary_text}}」 in the largest type, high contrast, thick weight, and clear spacing. The salary must be readable at thumbnail size.
- Lower area: two or three short benefit chips only, such as 「未経験OK」, 「WワークOK」, 「在宅相談可」, or role-appropriate equivalents. Keep them short and consistent with the post text.
- Use diagonal black separators and colored bands to create motion, but keep the reading order obvious: role -> salary -> benefits.
- Preserve enough margin around Japanese text so strokes do not touch edges or overlap the person.

Typography:
- Bold, clean Japanese gothic display typography.
- Use very large numerals for the salary and thick black supporting text.
- Keep text crisp, high contrast, and legible on a phone.
- Do not use tiny explanatory text, long sentences, decorative script, broken Japanese, or warped text.

Text to include:
- Main role: 「{{role_phrase}}」
- Salary: 「{{salary_text}}」
- Short benefit: 「未経験OK」
- Optional small context label: 「{{region}}」 only when appropriate and not misleading.

Avoid:
- Do not make 「クリックして」, 「ボタンで」, 「LINEで」, or any button-like CTA the main message.
- Do not include LINE URLs, QR codes, real company names, real logos, watermarks, or contact information.
- Do not contradict the job post details.
- Do not create SVG, HTML, XML, code output, flat vector art, line art, icon-only art, Figma/Canva-style shape templates, or a banner that looks like it is made only from geometric panels.
- Do not rely on icons as the main visual. Any icons must be small support elements only.
- Do not use low contrast, blurry text, excessive small copy, or clutter that makes the salary hard to read.

Output requirements:
- Generate a raster PNG image.
- Prioritize the recruitment appeal of the image itself over CTA-style instruction text.
- Keep the final banner vivid, readable, trustworthy, and suitable for future JMTY job postings using the placeholders.
