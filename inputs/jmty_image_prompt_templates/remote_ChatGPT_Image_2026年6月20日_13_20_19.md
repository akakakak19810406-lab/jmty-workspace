Use case: ads-marketing
Asset type: 1:1 Japanese job recruitment banner for Jimoty

Create a high-impact Japanese recruitment banner for:
「{{role_phrase}}」
「{{salary_text}}」
「{{region}}」

Main copy:
- Most prominent salary text: 「{{salary_text}}」
- Role headline: 「{{role_phrase}}」
- Short support copy: 「未経験OK」
- Optional area cue only if it matches the post: 「{{region}}」

Visual style:
- Energetic semi-realistic raster illustration or photo-real composite, not vector art.
- Bright, vivid recruitment-ad look with strong hot pink and teal contrast.
- A friendly, modern Japanese worker or candidate as the main visual, shown in a realistic workspace or home-work setting with depth, light, hair/fabric texture, and environmental detail.
- Use dynamic paint-brush strokes, ink splashes, and rough painted accents as background energy, but keep them behind the text and subject.
- The image should feel bold, youthful, trustworthy, and readable on a phone.

Layout:
- Square 1:1 composition.
- Place the person or work scene on one side or slightly behind the typography, with the face and expression clear.
- Make 「{{salary_text}}」 the largest element, in thick white Japanese/number typography with strong shadow, teal/pink offset, or 3D-like depth for contrast.
- Put 「{{role_phrase}}」 near the upper area as a large readable headline.
- Use 1-2 short benefit labels such as 「未経験OK」 or a post-consistent work-style phrase, set on rough white brush panels or clean high-contrast text areas.
- If 「{{region}}」 is used, keep it smaller than the salary and role text; do not let the region become the main appeal.
- Add a simple bottom information strip only if useful, using concise text and high contrast. Keep it secondary.

Typography:
- Bold, clean Japanese display type with thick strokes.
- Salary numbers must be oversized, crisp, and readable at smartphone thumbnail size.
- Keep all Japanese text short. Avoid dense paragraphs and tiny notes.
- Maintain strong contrast between text and background. Use white text with teal/pink shadows or teal text on white brush panels.

Color and density:
- Dominant colors: hot pink, teal, white, with small darker shadows for depth.
- High saturation and glossy ad-like lighting are acceptable, but avoid muddy colors.
- Keep the composition lively and dense enough to feel like a job banner, while preserving clear text hierarchy.

Required output rules:
- Generate a raster PNG-style image with sharp, legible text.
- Prioritize the visual appeal of the banner itself.
- Do not include LINE URLs, QR codes, real company names, logos, watermarks, or fake app UI.
- Do not use strong button-like CTA wording such as 「クリックして」, 「ボタンで」, 「LINEで」 as the main appeal.
- Do not contradict the post text. Use only claims that fit the provided role, salary, and region.

Avoid:
- SVG, vector art, flat illustration, line art, icon-only graphics, Figma/Canva-style geometric ad templates, or designs that look like pure shapes even if exported as PNG/JPG.
- A banner made only from colored panels, icons, and text without photographic or semi-real environmental texture.
- Tiny text, cluttered disclaimers, unreadable Japanese, broken typography, cropped salary numbers, overlaid text on busy face details, or low-contrast copy.
