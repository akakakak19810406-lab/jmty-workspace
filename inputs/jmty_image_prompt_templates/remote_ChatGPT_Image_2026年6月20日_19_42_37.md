Use case: ads-marketing
Asset type: 1:1 Japanese job recruitment banner for Jimoty

Create a reusable Japanese job banner in the same visual language as a premium, high-density 3D recruitment ad. The image should feel energetic, trustworthy, approachable, and easy to understand at smartphone size.

Required Japanese text:
- Main role phrase: 「{{role_phrase}}」
- Primary salary text, shown largest and with highest contrast: 「{{salary_text}}」
- Region or work area: 「{{region}}」
- Short support phrase: 「未経験OK」
- Optional short benefit labels: 「在宅相談可」「WワークOK」「スキマ時間も活用」

Suggested content hierarchy:
1. Put 「{{salary_text}}」 as the strongest focal point, using very large, bold, glossy 3D lettering with strong contrast.
2. Place 「{{role_phrase}}」 near the top or upper-left as the job category headline.
3. Show 「{{region}}」 as a smaller but readable location badge or panel.
4. Add 2-3 short benefit panels only if they fit clearly on mobile.
5. Keep all text short, thick, and legible; avoid long sentences.

Visual style:
- Square 1:1 raster image, polished Japanese recruitment banner mood.
- Semi-realistic or toy-like 3D commercial rendering with real lighting, depth, shadows, and material texture.
- Use a warm indoor work scene or believable job-support scene in the background, such as a desk, laptop, office corner, or home work area.
- Include one friendly central person or character-like worker figure to create approachability, but keep the salary and job text more important than the character.
- Use dense but organized composition: large central salary block, strong headline area, right-side or lower-side benefit panels, and small decorative highlights.
- Prefer glossy gold/yellow for the salary numbers, deep navy or dark blue signboard areas, white headline text with dark drop shadows, red accent burst for 「未経験OK」, and green/orange/purple accent panels for benefits.
- Typography should feel like bold Japanese advertising: thick, dimensional, high-contrast, outlined, with clear shadows and highlights.
- Background must have photographic or semi-realistic depth and texture; do not make a flat template of simple shapes.

Layout guidance:
- Make the salary area the first thing the viewer notices.
- Use layered signboards, badges, ribbons, and panels as supporting elements, not as the whole image.
- Keep enough spacing around text so Japanese characters do not merge or break.
- Use high contrast between text and background; text must remain sharp after resizing.
- Balance excitement with credibility; do not make the image look like a game-only poster if the job offer is general.

Prompt placeholders:
- {{role_phrase}} example: 求人サポートスタッフ
- {{salary_text}} example: 月収40万円目安
- {{region}} example: 青葉県
- {{main_copy}} example: 求人サポートスタッフ / 月収40万円目安 / 未経験OK
- {{scene}} example: clean Japanese recruitment banner mood, approachable and trustworthy

Avoid:
- Do not use 「クリックして」「ボタンで」「LINEで」 or other strong button-like CTA wording as the main message.
- Do not include LINE URLs, QR codes, real company names, logos, watermarks, or misleading claims.
- Do not make the banner text contradict the related job post.
- Do not use tiny text, thin fonts, broken Japanese typography, unreadable decorations, or excessive small labels.
- Do not generate SVG, vector art, flat illustration, line art, icon-based banners, shape-only templates, or Figma/Canva-style vector ads.
- Even if the file is PNG or JPG, the visual must not look like an SVG/vector/shape-only ad.
- Do not create HTML, SVG, XML, code output, or a layout made only from geometric shapes.
- Do not let decorative characters, icons, or props overpower 「{{salary_text}}」.

Output requirement:
- Generate as a raster PNG image.
- Prioritize crisp text, high contrast, and the recruitment appeal of the image itself.
